## Mother — Karen Coakley

The most complex relationship in the record. Early life: Karen was emotionally unstable, possibly undiagnosed bipolar, struggled with significant depression and anxiety during and after the divorce. She left Nick alone on Christmas (left sick for the Bahamas while he watched Harry Potter). She enrolled him in Teen Challenge at 16 without warning — Nick felt she did it out of her conflict with his father, not purely for his wellbeing (a letter from that period reads "I feel great!" which Nick calls "fake god shit"). She cut his college funding over substance use, skipped classes, and selling drugs. The relationship was marked by rupture after rupture.

And then repair. By 2023, Nick describes her as "incredibly supportive" (sometimes suffocatingly so). By 2026, she is his closest confidant. He practices boundaries with her (she talks too much, she is emotionally demanding; Janice advised "You are not your mother's therapist"), but the love is deep and acknowledged. He says he wants to "bless my mother for all that she has done for me." The evolution: from seeing her as a narcissist whose boundaries he researched (2022, copied articles about narcissistic mothers) to recognizing her as a flawed human who did her best and became his rock. After the January 2026 breakup, "Mom finally came" (Jan 12) — she remains active support. Mom is also involved in troubleshooting his gut health issues.

## Father — Steve Labo

Absent ages 6-16. Reconnected at Teen Challenge (the holiday-release hiding incident was the first reunion in nearly 10 years). Now stable but not emotionally intimate. Lives in South Carolina. Overweight with associated health issues; doesn't take care of his health. Nick describes him as someone who lets things bounce off, does not take life too seriously, "grabs himself by the balls and keeps going" — thick-skinned, doesn't overthink. Nick also documents embarrassment about his father's behavior: crude humor about women, aggressive physical greetings with girlfriends. Nick fears becoming his father and fears his partners judging him by his father. In September 2024, Nick introduced Karly to his father and privately processed shame about his father's behavior and appearance.

## Family Dynamic and Repair

Nick's family system was fractured at a formative age and has required significant repair work across adulthood. The mother-son relationship has undergone the most dramatic arc: from deep rupture and mistrust (Teen Challenge) to current status as closest confidant. This pattern of rupture-to-repair repeats across Nick's relationships generally.

## The Family Nick Wants to Build

Not the family he came from, but the family he intends to build. The drive to be a great father and husband appears in every goal document from 2020 through 2026. Christmas alone watching Harry Potter is the wound; a house full of kids and traditions is the antidote. Ideal family: 2 kids, strong traditions, holiday culture, travel, deep involvement in the kids' lives. Parenting philosophy: tough love with deep involvement; he uses Buddy's care as a proxy for future parenting ("father training"). Timeline historically stated as family within 5-6 years of each planning cycle. An open question he carries: "Can I forgive my mother fully and release the origin wound?" — the relationship has improved dramatically, but the childhood material still generates charge and full integration has not occurred.
