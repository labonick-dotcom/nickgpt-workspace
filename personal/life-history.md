## Childhood and Family Origin (Age 0-16)

Nick was born March 29, 1992 in the Boston area. His parents divorced when he was **around four or five years old** (confirmed by Nick, June 8 2026) after his father had an affair (with a nanny or secretary). The custody battle that followed split the family. His older brothers Chris and Steve/Stevie went with their father. Nick and his sister Jenna moved to Florida with their mother Karen. His father was largely absent from ages six through sixteen — the family was largely separated for nearly 10 years until the reunion around Teen Challenge (~16).

The childhood was chaotic. Karen struggled with depression and anxiety, possibly undiagnosed bipolar disorder. Nick moved between relatives' homes. He describes the family system as "marked by instability, emotional inconsistency, and divided loyalties." The extended family in Boston (aunts, uncles) provided crucial stability when they could. As the youngest child, Nick experienced repeated attachment disruptions, abandonment, and inconsistent caregiving.

**Core Wound Statement (self-authored in 2026 therapy intake):** "As the youngest child, I experienced repeated attachment disruptions, abandonment, and lack of continuity in caregiving. These dynamics have had lasting effects on my sense of security and my relationships, often showing up as swings between anxious and avoidant attachment patterns."

A formative memory: spending Christmas alone watching Harry Potter while his mother left sick for the Bahamas. This image returns in his writing across multiple years. It anchors his drive to build a healthy family and never let his future children feel that kind of alone.

Nick was a dysregulated kid. He acted out, got suspended, clashed with authority. By his mid-teens he was drinking and partying heavily.

## Teen Challenge (Age ~16)

At approximately age 16, his mother secretly enrolled him in Teen Challenge, a 15-month faith-based residential program for life-controlling behaviors (substance-related and behavioral concerns). He references this as "bootcamp" in some entries. His father, reconnecting after a decade of absence, tried to intervene legally. During a holiday release from the program, Nick's father helped him avoid returning, leading to a month of hiding before he turned himself in. This was also the first reunion with his father in nearly 10 years.

Nick was profoundly impacted, describing it as a major rupture in his relationship with his mother. He felt she sent him away out of her conflict with his father, not purely for his wellbeing. A letter from this period shows her writing "I feel great!" — which Nick identifies as "fake god shit." He completed high school online during the program. After leaving, Nick returned to Florida more rebellious and extreme, continuing to party through high school and into college.

Teen Challenge coincided with his father's reappearance and was a major turning point, though the full impact took years to metabolize.

## College Years (FGCU)

College was at Florida Gulf Coast University. The early years were rough — more partying, more consequences. His sophomore year, his mother cut financial support because of continued substance use, skipped classes, and selling drugs. This was a major pivot.

His junior year something shifted. He changed his major from marketing to finance, put himself through school, repaired the relationship with his mother, studied abroad in France, and began intensive personal development and therapy. France was the first time he felt genuinely expanded by an experience rather than running from one. This marked the beginning of his long-term commitment to growth. He graduated in 2015.
