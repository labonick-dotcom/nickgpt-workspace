# Nick Labo — Leadership and the IgKnight Brotherhood

> Summary: Nick Labo's involvement in IgKnight the King men's brotherhood (~2023-2026) — the four archetype seasons (Lover, Warrior, Magician, King), King Immersion retreats, the Gold Round standing ovation (May 2024), the Wolf bonfire conflict, leaving over the Michael/Russell financial dispute (Nov 2024), re-engaging for the Sedona Magician immersion (2026), and his identity as a "lighthouse of truth" and emerging leader of men. Mentor Michael Diamond's feedback is here and in friendships-and-support-network.md.

## Overview

IgKnight the King is a men's development/brotherhood organization of approximately 15 men, centered on immersive retreats. Nick has been involved for ~3 years. Weekly virtual "bonfire" check-ins for accountability and growth; quarterly immersions (originally Austin; later Sedona) where phones are turned in and intensive personal work happens. The brotherhood has provided Nick the community, accountability, and male intimacy he never had — the single most influential accountability structure in his life outside therapy. Onboarding insight he held onto: "The thought of being alone can be terrifying, because for us it is terrifying. You will not be alone anymore with the brotherhood."

His named accountability brothers: Daniel, Alex, Trey, Sway, Wolf, PJ, Russell, Michael. Other figures: Gary, Sam, Spencer, Zarek, James.

## The Four Archetype Seasons

If 80% of your relationship isn't joyful and fun, that's a red flag." Michael: "First things first — address the relationship." Major work: breaking up with Lauren, moving to Florida. Lover questionnaire — main desire: "Utmost security, confidence, balance, and peace within myself." Weeds in the garden: "Substance abuse: pot, uppers, nicotine. Womanizing, flapping." Most scared of: "Letting go of relationships that no longer serve me, especially with my current girlfriend." Outcome: "Face my addicted lover head on." End of season: "Uncomfortably growing."

**Season 2 — The Warrior (Fall 2023, November 1-5).** Pre-retreat state: 4 months into Florida, 5 months post-Lauren, "6 out of 10." Format: outdoor camping, cold plunge, physical challenges (taser, knife/stick fight training with Tracy). Nick initially avoided the knife/taser challenges; on Sunday reversed and fully committed: "I was legit in beast mode, fuck it let's go! I wanted blood. I am SO fucking happy I did that." Led a Wim Hof session ("a big deal" per Michael). James and Michael: "The discipline you have — there's no chance you're a drug addict." Takeaway: "I really witnessed the magic of this brotherhood and appreciated the value it brings to my life." Deep-dive feedback from Michael: "As long as you need to rely on a substance to get through the day, you'll never believe that YOU are enough" and "IDENTITY CHANGE — that's major reconstruction surgery."

**Season 3 — The Magician (Early 2024).** Pre-retreat state: "Happy, driven, motivated, feeling balanced and on a mission." Claiming: "Emerging as my most powerful, energized, confident self... I'm no longer desperately searching for my purpose, but living out every day with purpose." Biggest challenge: "Loneliness and the desire for a life partner... the life clock is ticking louder and faster than ever." Sober January 2024 broke with a drink on a date → Wolf conflict.

**Season 4 — The King (May 2024).** King Retreat #2 (May 16-19, 2024). Received the "Gold Round" — extended peer feedback acknowledging his internal strength, authenticity, potential as a speaker, humor as medicine, and fight with addiction. Standing ovation described; multiple brothers independently compared him to Neo believing in The Matrix. "He's starting to believe." Committed to sobriety here. ## The Wolf Bonfire Conflict (January-March 2024)

Nick posted a Sober January check-in admitting he'd had a white claw on a date, breaking the commitment. Wolf responded critically and publicly. Nick experienced it as harsh, critical, performative, and unsafe. He processed extensively with Michael, then sent Wolf a direct private message acknowledging impact but pushing back on delivery. He used an 11-step accountability process (identify agreement, prioritize consciously, consider impact, challenge negative beliefs, set new agreement, seek support) and committed to a "Reflective March."

Nick's message to Wolf: "I appreciate that you are reading my check-ins this month. Your feedback is usually helpful and valuable to me. But I'm not posting for your approval and I didn't ask for your coaching or chastising. In the future, if you have burning feedback for me, I ask that you come to me personally." Nick's takeaway: "My actions may have conveyed a message to the brotherhood that I do not take our collective commitments seriously" — and he linked his reaction (being judged by authority without explanation) to childhood. Request to Michael: "I struggle with feeling safe enough to share raw and real that may fall short of my standards... I don't do well with public call-outs."
