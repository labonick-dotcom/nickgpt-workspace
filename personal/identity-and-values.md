# Nick Labo — Identity and Values

> Summary: Nick Labo's self-concept, named identities (Little Nicky, NEON, King Nick, Second Floor Nick, Sacred Third), core values hierarchy (authenticity, family, growth, freedom, service), self-labels, and what he rejects. Spans 2020-2026, including Janice's "light up the world or burn it to the ground" framing and his foundational 2026 goal to stop escaping his internal state.

## Who Nick Is (Executive Snapshot)

Nick Labo is a 34-year-old enterprise sales executive at Microsoft, living in St. Petersburg, Florida, with his dog Buddy. He sells Dynamics 365, Power Platform, and Copilot Studio. He is building an AI consulting business (NickGPT) on the side. He grew up in a fractured New England family, was placed in a faith-based residential program at 16, put himself through college at FGCU, studied abroad in France, and climbed from Gartner to Salesforce to Microsoft over a decade. He earned his MBA from LSU Shreveport while working full-time.

Nick's defining tension is between extraordinary energy and the impulse to escape his own internal state. He completed an 8-month Adderall detox in 2025 and is rebuilding his identity without stimulants. He ended a 1.5-year relationship with Karly in January 2026 after a 3-year relationship with Lauren ended in June 2023.

He is deeply invested in personal growth: breathwork, jiu jitsu, men's brotherhood (IgKnight the King), therapy, biohacking, and spiritual practice. His stated foundational goal for 2026 is to become someone who no longer needs to escape his internal state to function. He cares about family, authenticity, helping others, and leaving a legacy. His therapist Janice told him he can either light up the world with his energy or burn it to the ground. He is choosing to light it up.

## Named Identities (Built and Discarded)

Nick has built, named, and discarded more identities in six years than most people try in a lifetime.

**"Little Nicky" (lifelong, named ~2022).** The wounded inner child. Abandonment trauma, fear of not being good enough, reactive, scared. Nick references Little Nicky in therapy notes as the part of himself that drives jealousy, substance use, and people-pleasing. He describes the dynamic as "Big Nick moving the ship and still carrying Little Nicky inside who houses the pain."

**"Second Floor Nick" (2020-2022, Dallas era; revived 2026).** An aspirational identity tied to his goal-setting and future authoring work. The name references elevation, getting above his baseline. This is Nick in planning mode: vision boards, affirmations, detailed life roadmaps. Revived as his 2026 anchor: "I AM SECOND-FLOOR NICK. I don't chase — I choose. Regulated. Clear. Unshakable. Comfort is not the goal — capacity is. No more band-aids. I hold the wound."

**"NEON" (2023, coined by therapist Janice).** Janice told him: "Nick, this is really who you are. You have a lot of energy, zest, passions, interests. Look at your whole family, y'all all have a lot of energy. It doesn't have to be a bad thing, it's your biggest challenge and value." She cautioned him against dimming his volume for a partner. This label stuck because it named what he had been told was a problem and reframed it as identity.

**"King Nick" (2024-present).** The brotherhood identity. Tied to his work with IgKnight the King. King Nick is the man who shows up vulnerably, leads other men, pursues sobriety, and claims his authentic voice. The name appears in journal headers throughout 2024-2025. At first aspirational; by late 2025, functioning more as a checkpoint ("how does King Nick feel?") than a costume.

**"Sacred Third" (2023-2024).** A concept Nick developed for his post-Lauren identity: sober, micro-dosing intentionally, enlightened, in tune with his inner voice. This was the bridge between substance-dependent Nick and fully sober Nick. By 2025, the micro-dosing component faded and the concept evolved into something closer to "naturally regulated." It is the version of himself that emerges when he is sober, present, and connected to his inner voice — not the substance-fueled performer, not the depressed baseline, but the third thing that emerges when the noise is removed.

**"The Court Jester" (2024, self-identified).** Nick recognizes his humor functions as medicine for others and defense for himself. Brotherhood members consistently name his humor as one of his most powerful traits. He uses it to disarm, connect, and deflect vulnerability in equal measure.

**Nick 1.0 / 2.0 / Current (ChatGPT synthesis framework).**
- **Nick 1.0**: Survival-based, emotionally dysregulated, self-reliant. Built intensity, hunger, emotional depth, need to create his own safety.
- **Nick 2.0**: The self-improvement warrior. Builds routines, therapy, breathwork, goal systems. Still carries an implicit "I am damaged and need fixing" frame. Improves but cycles.
- **Current Nick**: At an inflection point. More honest, less interested in symptom management, more interested in identity coherence. Trying to move from "managing volatility" to "becoming stable enough to hold real life."

## Core Values and Operating Principles

Nick has listed his values explicitly in multiple files. Cross-referencing stated values with demonstrated behavior produces this hierarchy.

**Tier 1: Non-Negotiables**

- **Authenticity.** The value Nick references most often and most intensely. "I give up being perfect for being perfectly authentic." He would rather be disliked for who he is than liked for a performance. His career setbacks (Gartner, Qualtrics) came from authenticity without wisdom; his growth has been learning to be real without being reckless.
- **Family.** Not the family he came from, but the family he intends to build. The drive to be a great father and husband appears in every goal document from 2020 through 2026. Christmas alone watching Harry Potter is the wound. A house full of kids and traditions is the antidote.
- **Growth.** Nick cannot be static. Stagnation is existentially threatening to him. He measures his worth partly by whether he is evolving. This value has a shadow side: it can become a treadmill where he is never enough as he currently is.

**Tier 2: Deeply Held**

- **Freedom.** Financial freedom, emotional freedom, freedom from substances, freedom to be himself in relationships. "From prison to fucken freedom" is not just about Lauren. It is about everything.
- **Service / Giving Back.** "Selfish to be selfless" is a load-bearing phrase in his operating system. He wants to accumulate power, money, and wisdom specifically so he can deploy them for others. Five years of volunteering with All Things Made New is evidence this is not aspirational only.
- **Integrity / Honesty.** Nick values truth-telling even when it costs him. He documents his own dishonesty in relationships (gaslighting Lauren, withholding from Karly) not to excuse it but to confront it. He respects people who are straight with him.
- **Health.** Physical and psychological optimization. Biohacking, breathwork, jiu jitsu, cold plunge, sauna, supplements, sleep tracking. Health is both a value and a coping mechanism. When he is healthy, everything else works. When he neglects it, things unravel.

**Tier 3: Important, Still Evolving**

- **Spirituality / Faith.** Nick returned to Christianity in the IgKnight era. His relationship with God is reflective, evolving, and non-rigid. He is drawn to humility, surrender, and moral grounding. He is also learning to distinguish genuine faith from spiritual bypassing.
- **Legacy / Impact.** He wants to be remembered. He wants to leave something. The form keeps shifting (professor, AI consultant, speaker, coach, psychologist), but the drive is constant.
- **Humor / Joy.** Being unapologetically fun-loving is a core identity claim. He does not want a heavy, serious life. He wants to be the guy who makes the room lighter.

**Operating Principles (Behavioral, Not Just Stated):**

- "If you do the things you need to do when you need to do them, then someday you can do the things you want to do when you want to do them."
- "Regret weighs pounds, discipline weighs ounces."
- "My mess equals my message."
- "Would you rather have your relationship with substances or a relationship with your family?" (Janice's question, adopted as personal principle)
- "Don't put artificial timelines on things. Surrender. Trust." (Michael Diamond's guidance, increasingly internalized)

## What Nick Is Not / What He Rejects

**I am not moderate.** Janice told him he would never land on moderation, and he agrees. He is all in or checked out. This is a feature and a bug.

**I reject dimming my energy for someone else's comfort.** Janice's intervention about Lauren: "Do you really want to change who you are to be with someone?" The answer, eventually, was no. He has committed to finding people (partners, friends, colleagues) who can match or welcome his intensity rather than asking him to reduce it.

**I reject the "addict" label as a permanent identity.** Nick has explicitly said: "I don't look at myself as an addict." He sees his substance use as a nervous system regulation strategy rooted in childhood trauma, not a lifelong disease identity. (Note: Ian Koniak's framing pushes the other direction — see health-and-substances. This is a conscious choice that may evolve.)

**I reject staying in relationships out of obligation.** Both breakups (Lauren, Karly) taught him the cost of ignoring his gut. "I will never ever sacrifice who the fuck I am and what I want to do to anyone."

**I reject corporate conformity as a life path.** He stays at Microsoft for the stability, but his identity is not "Microsoft employee." He is building toward independence.

**I reject passive, non-growth-oriented living.** Stagnation in a partner is a dealbreaker. Stagnation in himself is existentially threatening. He judges harshly (in himself and others) the choice to coast.

**I reject the version of myself that runs from discomfort.** This is aspirational but increasingly real. The Adderall detox was the hardest test of this rejection.

**Identities he has moved away from:**
- Party guy / chaos-seeker (teens through mid-20s)
- The person who needs substances to function (2020-2024 transition)
- The boyfriend who stays too long (learned through Lauren and Karly)
- The people-pleaser who avoids confrontation (ongoing work)
