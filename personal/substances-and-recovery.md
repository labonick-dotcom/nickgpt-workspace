# Nick Labo — Substances and Recovery

> Summary: Nick Labo's central life arc with substances — 15+ years of Adderall (peak ~90mg/day), daily marijuana, nicotine, kratom, periodic cocaine; the 2025 Adderall detox (~April–December 2025) and December 17, 2025 relapse, after which he has gradually increased and is now at 60mg/day (current/ongoing, not a resolved recovery); therapeutic ketamine (Joyous) and the February 2026 Sedona DMT immersion; plus key quotes and milestones. Covers 2008–June 2026.

## The Central Arc

This is not a sidebar in Nick's story — it is the spine. He has used substances to regulate his nervous system since his teens, and untangling that dependency has driven more personal growth than any other single factor. Janice's framing (adopted by Nick): "You never learned to self-soothe. Your nervous system got jacked up early, so it makes sense you rely on marijuana to get that self-soothing."

Nick rejects the permanent "addict" label: "I don't look at myself as an addict." He frames his use as a nervous-system regulation strategy rooted in childhood trauma. (Ian Koniak's counter-framing: "Once you realize you are in fact an addict, and that the chase to feel will never go away — that's when you'll gain your power. We have low dopamine. I'll always be looking for a spike. Learn to be okay without the spike.")

## ADHD and Dopamine Architecture

Nick has ADHD, diagnosed. Enneagram Type 7 (The Enthusiast): sensation-seeking, future-oriented, pain-avoidant, drawn to stimulation. The brotherhood helped him see all his vices (Adderall, porn, novelty, chaos, intensity) as dopamine-seeking patterns, not moral failures. The Adderall detox forced him to experience life at his natural dopamine baseline for the first time in 15 years — brutal weeks of doing nothing, watching Netflix, playing computer games, hiding from work. His growth edge: building a life that generates enough natural dopamine (purpose, fitness, connection, creation) that he doesn't need chemical supplementation.

## Open Questions
