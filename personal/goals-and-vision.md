## Mental Models and Frameworks

**"Selfish to be Selfless."** Nick's operating philosophy for wealth, health, and achievement. You have to build yourself up first (money, stability, emotional regulation, physical health) before you can effectively serve others. Appears in nearly every goal document from 2020 forward.

**The 9 Pillars / Ceiling Framework (2022).** Imagine you are a ceiling held up by 9 pillars: Contribution, Hobbies/Leisure, Exercise, Family, Alone Time, Personal Growth, Work, Relationship/Relationships, Friends. If one falls, you have eight others. If you neglect all but two, the ceiling crashes when one gives way. A recurring pattern is over-investing in one pillar (e.g., relationships) at the expense of others (e.g., mission, gym, social). Nick uses this to evaluate life balance.

**Emotional Bank Account.** Borrowed from Stephen Covey. Nick references protecting his "emotional bank account from unnecessary withdrawals." Applied to relationships, social obligations, and energy management.

**A/B/C People Framework.** A people build you up and make you feel better after contact. B people are neutral. C people drain you. Spend more time with As, phase out Cs. Applied to social network pruning.

**Equanimity as North Star.** Nick's stated "main goal" across all future authoring exercises is equanimity — psychological and spiritual stability, ability to return to center under any condition. "Silent observer of my own mind" is the long-term target state.

**"Wound is Also Your Gift."** His mess becomes his message. The pain of growing up in chaos, battling addiction, and navigating broken relationships is not wasted if he uses it to help others.

**"What's the Difference Between Fear and Excitement? The Only Difference Is the Story."** A reframing tool for anxiety. The physiological sensation is the same. The narrative determines the experience.

**"Regret Weighs Pounds, Discipline Weighs Ounces."** Internalized maxim about the asymmetric cost of inaction vs. action.
