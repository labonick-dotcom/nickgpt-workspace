## WHAT "BEING SEEN" ACTUALLY MEANS

You've gotten this feedback repeatedly and it doesn't fully land. Here's the breakdown.

**It's not about sharing more.**
You share. You talk. You can articulate your inner life well. That's not the gap. The gap isn't the content, it's the management underneath the content.

**It's about showing up unmanaged.**
When you're regulated - on Adderall, behind the performance, in your head monitoring how you're landing - people are getting a pre-processed Nick. Cleaned up. Ready. They feel there's more underneath, but the door stays closed. The immersion moment Mike E. referenced, where you finally started opening up about childhood trauma - that was probably a moment you stopped managing how you came across and just landed.

**The parking brake is self-monitoring.**
You're watching yourself as you show up. Editing in real time. That surveillance keeps people at arm's length. You're present, you're engaged, but you're not fully in the room. Michael named it across the whole season: "a lack of permission to feel, to receive, to be seen, to stop hiding."

**The chemical piece is directly connected.**
You said it yourself: "chemically muting myself." Adderall and the escapism aren't just health issues - they're relational ones. Chemical regulation is a form of self-management. When you're managing your internal experience, people get a controlled version. The rawness that would actually connect - the uncertainty under the confidence, the unfinished edges - gets filed down before anyone can touch it.

**Your gifts are part of what you're hiding.**
This is the golden shadow Michael framed the whole season around. It's not just wounds you're concealing. You're hiding your actual size. Mike P. sees stages. Gary says "unbelievable." Frank says the lightness plus the dark, embodied together with aliveness, is "what's going to pop." They're not describing a fixed future Nick. They're describing the current Nick if the brake came off.

**The trauma weight is the specific hiding mechanism.**
Mike E. named it: you've been carrying the weight so long it made you strong. But you're still bracing against it, still managing around it, still making sure it doesn't show. Letting yourself be seen means letting people see you've carried something heavy - not as a confession, but as a fact of who you are. The strength that came from it becomes visible when you stop hiding that it happened.

**What it looks like in practice:**
Not more sharing. Not vulnerability theater. Stop arriving pre-packaged. Let a thought land before you've polished it. Let people see the hesitation before the confidence. Let yourself be in the room without also watching yourself be in the room.

This call was actually a live example. You went first. You named the drugs without softening it. You said "I know it's that big." Michael felt the energy shift in real time and named it. That's what it looks like.
