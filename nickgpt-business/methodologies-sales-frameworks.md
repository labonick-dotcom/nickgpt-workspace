## AI Maturity Classification System (4 Tiers)

Every coaching client is classified into one of four tiers from their AI audit output, intake form, and LinkedIn analysis. The tier drives the session arc, build scope, and coaching approach.

- **Tier 1 (AI Unaware):** No meaningful AI usage; may have tried ChatGPT once. Session: identity shift, live demonstration, first-use "aha" moment. Build: starter project, basic prompts for top 3 use cases.
- **Tier 2 (AI Casual):** Uses AI sporadically for simple tasks; no system. Session: reframe from tool to thinking partner, demonstrate one workflow. Build: structured project, custom instructions, 5 purpose-built prompts.
- **Tier 3 (AI Competent):** Regular user with some sophistication; iterates. Session: move from prompts to workflows, introduce automation thinking. Build: advanced project, workflow prompts, multiple context files.
- **Tier 4 (AI Advanced):** Power user; has built custom GPTs or systems. Session: optimize existing systems, introduce new capabilities. Build: system optimization, automation design, cross-platform workflows. **Flag: potential NickGPT implementation client at $3K-$5K.**

(Brad Figler sat at the Tier 3-4 boundary.)

## Workshop / Talk Frameworks

**The Influence Architecture Model.** Influence is what you architect before, during, and between meetings. Three parts: (1) Before the room — AI-powered prep and intelligence synthesis; (2) In the room — judgment-amplified conversation; (3) Between meetings — Phantom Advisor follow-up narrative and positioning.

**The AI Leverage Stack.** Four levels: (1) Research and synthesis (compress 4 hours into 20 minutes); (2) Narrative and positioning; (3) Preparation and confidence (role play, objection prep, meeting simulation); (4) System design (territory planning, account prioritization, execution discipline).

**Identity Before Tools.** Three parts: (1) The trap — AI as productivity vs leverage; (2) The shift — from user to orchestrator; (3) The path — specific systems, workflows, frameworks.
