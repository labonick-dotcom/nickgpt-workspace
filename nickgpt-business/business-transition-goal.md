# Business: The Transition Goal (Microsoft → NickGPT Full-Time)

> Summary: Nick's plan to eventually leave Microsoft and run NickGPT consulting as primary income — the goal, the financial threshold logic, Ian Koniak's advice (April 14 2026) that Microsoft stability is an asset until consulting can replace the income, and the revenue math that defines "replacement."

## The Goal

Nick is building the NickGPT consulting business **simultaneously with his Microsoft Enterprise AE role**. The explicit goal: **eventually leave Microsoft and run consulting as primary income.**

- **Timeline:** not fixed. Direction clear.
- Microsoft provides financial stability while the consulting business builds momentum. Nick has significant free time within his Microsoft role (he knows the job well) and invests it in consulting.

## The Trigger / Threshold (Replace the Income, Don't Abandon Early)

**Ian Koniak's stance (April 14, 2026):** the Microsoft stability is an asset for now, and the play is to **build consulting to where it can replace that income, not abandon it early.** Nick agrees.

So the trigger is financial replacement, not a fixed date. The relevant revenue math (the threshold to clear):

> **Revenue at capacity:** retainer $4,000 + 1:1 coaching at 15 hrs ($7,500 Nick's share) + external clients (2/month at target, $6,000-$8,000) = **$17,500-$19,500/month, $210,000-$234,000/year.**

That projected at-capacity figure ($210K-$234K/year) is the implicit income-replacement target the transition hinges on. (No single dollar threshold or hard date is stated in the source beyond this; the trigger is "consulting can replace Microsoft income.")

## How the Up-Market Shift Supports the Transition
