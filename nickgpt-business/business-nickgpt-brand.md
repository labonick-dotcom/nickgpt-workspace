## What NickGPT Is

NickGPT is Nick Labo's AI consulting business, built on the side of his Microsoft Enterprise AE role with a clear path to running it full time. It is also the name of his personal business Claude project / brand identity.

**The single most important thing about Nick's value:** he is not good at AI. He is good at **translating AI into real business leverage** for people who know what they want but cannot build it themselves.

How Nick describes what he does (plain language):
- "I build AI systems for sales coaches and professionals that eliminate the manual admin work so they can focus on what they are actually good at."
- "I take the tools you already have, Claude or ChatGPT, and organize them into a system that works without you having to think about it. You drop a transcript in. The summary writes itself. The follow-up email lands in your drafts. You review and send."

NickGPT is an operator's business — Nick built specific tools for a specific market and is productizing that experience into a repeatable service. (For positioning vs other AI consultants, see business-positioning.md.)

## Operating Principles (How to Run the Business)

- Work in focused conversations within the right project. Do not maintain one endless thread.
- Capture conversation outputs into knowledge files.
- Build playbooks with every engagement, not just deliverables.
- Price based on value, not time.
- Treat early clients as mutual investments.
- Be honest about capability boundaries.

**Build vs Run diagnostic:** every engagement splits into what must be *built* (an agent you watch and steer: Cowork or Claude Code) and what must *run* it (the delivered Project on the client's connector, optionally an always-on operator: OpenClaw or Make). Ask "what has to be built, and what has to run it," not "which tool is best."

**The Auth Boundary:** the agent has the hands and the speed; the human holds the keys. Build under read-only or staging, the human authorizes writes and keeps their own credentials. Read-only-first on any live client system.

**The Blueprint Is the Asset:** encode domain knowledge into a reusable build-skill once; any agent then runs it from a one-line prompt. The tool is interchangeable; the skill is the product. Cowork forges the skill, OpenClaw scales it.

**Everything Must Connect to Ian's Two Levers (for UYSP work):** (1) driving new meetings and conversions; (2) driving renewals. If a project connects to neither, it is not a current UYSP priority.
