## Pre-Visit Sedation Protocol (Established April 16, 2026)

Buddy has severe veterinary-visit anxiety with prior documented failure to draw blood due to reactivity. On April 16, 2026 this protocol succeeded:

- Gabapentin 400mg (4 × 100mg caps) at 10:30 AM
- Trazodone 150mg (3 × 50mg tabs) at 10:30 AM
- CBD 50mg (Lazarus Naturals Full Spectrum, normal daily dose) at 10:30 AM
- All given together with a small food portion (few tablespoons Viva Raw rabbit)
- Vet arrived 1:00 PM (2.5 hours post-dose). **Result: blood draw successful; sedation adequate.**

Notes for future visits:
- Ideal protocol adds a priming dose of gabapentin the night before (300mg at bedtime) in addition to the morning dose.
- Without night-before priming, 400mg morning gabapentin compensates but may produce slightly less sedation.
- Trazodone ceiling is 150mg per official prescription — do not exceed.
- CBD is additive/synergistic for sedation — include at normal dose.
- Give with food (trazodone can cause nausea on empty stomach).
- Dr. Dowling confirmed: short walks are safe while sedated; avoid long excursions; no jumping on furniture.
- Fresh medication is essential — expired gabapentin/trazodone (2+ years past expiration) provide 10–20% reduced potency at best.
- Log sedation level and draw success after each visit for protocol calibration.
