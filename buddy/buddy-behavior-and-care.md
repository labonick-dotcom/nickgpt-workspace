## Behavioral Overview

Buddy's behavioral issues were identified within months of adoption (June 2019) and have been the subject of sustained professional intervention across multiple trainers and a veterinary behaviorist from 2021–2023. Core issues:

1. **Attachment-specific separation distress centered on Nick** — distress spikes when Nick (primary owner) leaves; rated 6/10 severity by Beth; the trainer explicitly identified it as distinct from generic separation anxiety: it is attachment to Nick specifically.
2. **Generalized anxiety disorder** — chronic, pervasive anxiety across environmental triggers; hypervigilance.
3. **Dog-directed reactivity** — anxious/reactive toward unfamiliar dogs; hair raises; whimpering; has triggered other dogs to attack (3 documented incidents).
4. **Noise sensitivity** — shaking, cowering, fear response to loud noises, buses, trash trucks, lawn mowers, hose spray, music.

## How Buddy Does When Nick Is Gone — May 2026 Regression Episode (Owner-Reported)

A significant behavioral regression and feeding disruption — **behavioral in origin, not medical.**

**Timeline:**
- **Early–mid May 2026:** Back-to-back guests visited Nick at home. Buddy's routine was disrupted; he became anxious and began refusing breakfast, though continued eating dinner normally.
- **Weekend ~May 10–12, 2026:** Nick traveled out of town. Nick's mom watched Buddy and brought him to Nick's sister's house on back-to-back days — three children, another dog, a loud household. Buddy was extremely anxious (independently observed by Nick's mom) and barely ate dinner.
- **During Nick's absence:** Mom and sister introduced chicken and other human food mixed into meals to coax him to eat; sister spoon-fed him at least once.
- **Post-return (~May 14–19, 2026):** Buddy continued refusing food even with Nick home. Dinner delayed from normal 6:00 PM until 9:30–10:00 PM. Nick added chicken and Buddy ate, but not all of it. The next morning, Buddy knocked the bowl over to get directly to the chicken pieces, ignoring the rabbit.

**Analysis (textbook anxiety-driven inappetence reinforced into conditioned food refusal):**
1. *Anxiety inappetence:* routine disruption caused stress-related appetite suppression, especially mornings when anticipating Nick's departure — consistent with his attachment-specific separation distress (6/10 per Beth).
2. *Stressor stacking:* Nick's absence + transport to a high-arousal environment pushed Buddy past his recovery threshold; he cannot handle compounding stressors without decompression time.
3. *Reinforcement:* chicken + spoon-feeding taught Buddy that refusal produces dramatically better food AND intense human attention — the attention component is at least as reinforcing as the food upgrade for an attachment-driven dog.
4. *Conditioned refusal:* Buddy now actively refuses rabbit to hold out for chicken; bowl-knocking is goal-directed/strategic, not medical.

**Clinical flags ruled out:** Thyroid (May 2026 panel doesn't support hypothyroidism); Dental (April 2026 puppy-clean teeth); GI (no vomiting/diarrhea/stool changes; selective eating rules out nausea/true inappetence); Kidney (creatinine normal, SDMA excellent April 2026).

**Recommended protocol:**
- Discontinue chicken immediately (flagged protein; reinforces refusal cycle).
- Return to rabbit only. Offer 20 minutes; if uneaten, remove. No substitutions, no coaxing, no hovering.
- A healthy 76 lb dog can safely skip meals for 24–48 hours.
- Once the refusal pattern breaks (3–5 days), consider rotating in Viva Raw Beef for variety (nutritionist-recommended, no recalls, no cross-reactivity).
- Dr. Dowling endorsed intermittent fasting as acceptable if Buddy gets adequate calories in one meal/day: "Intermittent fasting, I don't think it's the worst thing in the world if he doesn't eat in the morning but he eats enough calories at night because he looked pretty good last time I saw him."
- If zero food intake exceeds 48 hours, or if vomiting/lethargy/stool changes appear, the medical differential reopens.

**Anxiety component:** Nick reports Buddy's anxiety is worse than historical baseline for comparable disruptions; Nick's mom independently confirmed heightened alertness/anxiety. Contributing factors: age-related decline in stress tolerance (8.7 yrs); off daily sertraline 2+ years (hydroxyzine/CBD provide some support but not equivalent to SSRI baseline); stressor stacking without decompression. **This may warrant a conversation with Dr. Dowling about reconsidering daily behavioral medication, or a veterinary behaviorist assessment (Courteous Canine, Lutz — Dr. Soraya Juarbe-Diaz).** Owner update during the May 2026 call: Buddy is "not as lethargic" and "walking again pretty normal" (improvement from the April "slowing down" report); morning food refusal attributed to schedule/anxiety, not medical; was "out of whack" while with Nick's mom during travel.

## Daily Routine (Established Protocol)

- **Morning:** Calm, low-key greeting; avoid excitement or "happy parties"; consider scatter feeding/food hunt as first activity to lower arousal.
- **Feeding:** Twice daily (rabbit patty base); food stored frozen, thawed in fridge; add water for hydration.
- **Walks:** Willing any time, any weather; leash required; use "Quick" cue for dog encounters; carry high-value treats; fanny pack with treats recommended.
- **Training/Enrichment (~20 min throughout day):** Nose work, scatter feeds, hide-and-seek, box games, touch cue, tug of war.
- **Playtime:** 10 min AM and PM; tug of war, tag, hide and seek, massage.
- **Evening:** Low arousal; relaxation protocol if applicable; consistent bedtime area.

## Sleep

- Typically: his own bed next to Nick's bed; sometimes foot of bed; occasionally sleeps alone in office.
- Restlessness at night documented during periods of high anxiety.

## Triggers to Manage

- **Departure cues** (shoes, keys, coat, fanny pack) — desensitize with door-as-bore.
- **Loud noises** (trash trucks, lawn mowers, hose spray, music) — counterconditioning protocol.
- **Other dogs on walks** — "Quick" cue; high-value treats ready.
- **New people / guests** — structured greeting; allow Buddy to approach on his terms.
- **Hot conditions** — monitor hydration; may refuse to drink when anxious even after exercise.
- **Stressor stacking** — Buddy cannot handle compounding stressors (guests + travel + chaotic household in rapid succession) without decompression time between them.

- **Enjoys:** tug of war, tag, hide and seek, massage, sniffing/nose work.

## Vet-Visit Anxiety and Sedation

Buddy has severe veterinary-visit anxiety with documented heart rates of 135–150 bpm at visits (NT-proBNP normal — "ragingly nervous when I come" per Dr. Dowling) and a prior failure to draw blood due to reactivity. The established pre-visit sedation protocol (gabapentin 400mg + trazodone 150mg + CBD 50mg, ~2.5 hours pre-visit, given with food) succeeded on April 16, 2026. Full protocol and dosing in buddy-health-medications.md.
