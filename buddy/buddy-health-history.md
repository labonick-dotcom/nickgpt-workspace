## The Thyroid Investigation (March 2020 – May 2026) — RESOLVED: Do Not Treat

For six years Buddy showed low or low-normal Total T4 with normal Free T4. Batch 4 (April 2026) framed a "THYROID CRISIS" when Zoetis returned both Total T4 (0.7 LOW) AND Free T4 (<0.3 LOW) for the first time, recommending immediate levothyroxine. **Batch 5 (May 2026) supersedes that framing.** A Premium Thyroid Panel at Michigan State University (gold standard, 7 values including first-ever TSH and autoantibodies) led Dr. Dowling — in consultation with a Zoetis internal medicine specialist — to decide **NOT to treat.**

**Complete thyroid trend (all 6 panels, 2020–May 2026) and the full numeric MSU panel breakdown are in buddy-health-labs.md.** Quick reference: Total T4 was low or low-normal at every draw except Feb 2025 (1.6, normal); the gold-standard May 2026 MSU panel returned TT4 7 nmol/L LOW, Free T4 6 pmol/L normal (equilibrium dialysis), first-ever TSH 0.28 ng/mL normal, and all autoantibodies negative → age-related decline, do not treat.

**Clinical reasoning (Dr. Dowling, w/ Zoetis specialist):** Normal TSH means the pituitary is not detecting a thyroid deficit (true primary hypothyroidism would show elevated TSH). No autoantibodies rules out autoimmune thyroiditis. Only 1 of 7 values out of range. Low-end values are expected for an aging dog (8.7–9 yrs). Supplementing would add unnecessary hepatic/renal processing burden without clear benefit. **Buddy is NOT on thyroid medication and the decision has been made not to initiate.** The April Zoetis Free T4 <0.3 ng/dL that drove the "crisis" was not confirmed by the more reliable MSU equilibrium dialysis (6 pmol/L). Verdict: age-related thyroid decline, not primary hypothyroidism. Monitor annually with the Premium Thyroid Panel (MSU). If TSH rises or Free T4 drops below the MSU floor on a future panel, the treatment question reopens.

> Hypothyroid-skin note: low-normal thyroid can contribute to a weaker skin barrier and local immune defenses (may worsen Malassezia colonization), but is no longer an actionable treatment lever.
